Visual Key Insights: 6


Non-Profit hospitals have consistently score on the higher end as they have no shareholder to please and only focus on giving the best services to their patients rather than making money.


Some states showed an increase in their available beds consistently through out the years data collected which showed growth and better accommodation for their patients.


Some regions showed rating score improvements consecutive years showing great patient care while also proving more of the disparity in health care quality from region to region


We saw a slight decrease in hospital to bed ratio in 2020 showing hospitals are cutting staff which is resulting in one employee having to take care of more patients that they used to which can impact health care quality and rating.


Most of the states have had a decrease in available beds which impacts the quality of health care services they offer their patients. This decrease in available beds is mostly seen on For-Profit hospitals which highlights on how budget could be the main reason for their decline.


The same states that have shown decrease in available hospital bed count are the ones that showed a decrease in health care quality score. This further explains the need for adequate accommodation and employee to bed ratio is important to make sure the patients are well taken care of.
