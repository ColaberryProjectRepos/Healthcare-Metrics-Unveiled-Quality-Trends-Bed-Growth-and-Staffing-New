Problem Statement 1

In 2021, a new high of 21% of Americans reported that the quality of healthcare is 'poor,' while 31% expressed that it is 'only fair.' This statistic highlights the critical issue in the United States healthcare system, revealing significant concerns about the quality of care. These issues persist across regions and hospital ownership types, as a thorough examination of data from 2017 to 2022 exposes glaring disparities. Geographic variations in healthcare quality are compounded by differences in public, private, and non-profit hospital performances. Annual shifts in hospital bed counts and staffing ratios further complicate the landscape. These disparities demand immediate attention, underscoring the urgency of implementing evidence-based strategies for equitable healthcare access and quality improvement
